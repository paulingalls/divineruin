# Post-Playtest Interview Guide

Conduct after the tester finishes all sessions. 15-20 minutes. Record audio.

The goal is to capture subjective experience that rubric scores miss. Let the tester talk — don't lead them to specific answers.

---

## Opening (2 min)

"Thanks for playing through the whole arc. I'm going to ask you some open-ended questions. There are no right answers — honest reactions help us the most."

---

## The DM (5 min)

1. **How would you describe the DM to someone who hasn't played?**
   *(Target: they describe a character/personality, not "an AI" or "a system")*

2. **Was there a moment where the DM surprised you?**
   *(Probing for improvisation quality)*

3. **Was there a moment where the DM felt robotic or broke immersion?**
   *(Capture specific failure modes)*

4. **Did the DM ever feel like it was reading your mood or energy correctly? Incorrectly?**
   *(Player Resonance System — Phase D calibration data)*

---

## The Companion (3 min)

5. **Tell me about Kael.**
   *(Do they talk about Kael as a person or as a game feature?)*

6. **Did Kael ever say or do something that made you feel something?**
   *(Emotional engagement signal)*

7. **Was Kael ever annoying or in the way?**
   *(Companion guidance balance)*

---

## Combat (3 min)

8. **Describe a combat encounter that stood out.**
   *(Can they narrate it back? Means it was memorable.)*

9. **Did you ever feel lost during combat — not knowing what you could do?**
   *(Audio-only combat clarity)*

10. **Did dice rolls feel meaningful?**
    *(Mechanical satisfaction)*

---

## The World (3 min)

11. **What do you think is going on with the artifact you found?**
    *(Mystery traction — are they theorizing?)*

12. **Was there a place that felt real to you?**
    *(Environmental narration quality)*

13. **Did the world feel like it existed when you weren't playing?**
    *(Async engagement impact)*

---

## The Verdict (3 min)

14. **If this was a game you could buy and play regularly, would you?**
    *(The 70% target question — record exact response)*

15. **What would need to change for that to be a definite yes?**
    *(Prioritization input)*

16. **Would you play this alone, with friends, or both?**
    *(Multiplayer demand signal)*

17. **Anything else you want to tell us?**

---

## Observer Notes During Interview

Record non-verbal cues: enthusiasm, hesitation, specific moments where they light up or grimace. These matter as much as the words.
